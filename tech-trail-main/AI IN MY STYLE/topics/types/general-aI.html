General Artificial Intelligence

Description

General AI refers to a type of Artificial Intelligence that possesses the ability to understand, learn, and apply knowledge across a wide range of tasks—just like a human. Unlike narrow AI, which is task-specific, General AI aims for cognitive flexibility, meaning it can reason, plan, solve problems, understand language, and even exhibit emotional intelligence across domains without being reprogrammed for each. It’s still a theoretical concept and not yet achieved in practice, but it represents the ultimate goal of many AI researchers.

Interview Questions with Answers

What is General AI (AGI)?

General AI refers to an intelligent system that can perform any intellectual task a human can. It possesses generalized cognitive abilities rather than narrow, task-specific intelligence.

Has General AI been achieved?

No. General AI is still theoretical. Most AI today is Narrow AI—built for specific tasks.

How is General AI different from Narrow AI?

Narrow AI performs one task efficiently (e.g., face recognition), while General AI can perform a wide variety of tasks without being retrained.

What challenges exist in building AGI?

Challenges include understanding consciousness, generalization across domains, emotional intelligence, and ethical decision-making.

Is ChatGPT a General AI?

No. ChatGPT is a form of narrow AI designed to process and generate human-like language. It cannot perform tasks beyond its training in a truly general way.
